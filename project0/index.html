Part 1: Selfie: The Wrong Way vs. The Right Way

Analysis

This is related to perspective distortion and focal length. When you are very close to the camera, the objects nearest to the lens (like the nose) appear disproportionately large. Stepping back and using zoom (telephoto effect) "compresses" facial features, making them look more natural and flatter, which is often considered a more flattering portrait effect.

Part 2: Architectural Perspective Compression

Analysis

Zoom in from a distance and the background slides right up behind your subject; far-off things swell and feel close. Go wide and the foreground balloons while the background shrinks away, giving the frame a sudden, deep breath of space.
